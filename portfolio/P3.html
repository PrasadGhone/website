Triggering Ride Sharing through socialization

Collaboration with Microsoft Research Work in Progress

Context

India is undergoing a major urbanisation. Rapid increase in population in urban areas demands improvement in transport services to ensure connectivity. The increase in population in metro cities is because of better employment. Growth in IT industries in cities like Pune, Mumbai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Delhi, etc. is evident. Every day lakhs of employees from these cities travel to these IT parks. Traffic is a burning problem of the roads connecting to IT parks. Lot of the productive time of these employees is wasted in traffic jams. An article by ‘The Hindu’ said, just in Bangalore, around 22k crore is lost per annum because of time lost in traffic.

Infrastructural change to solve these problems can be solution but the demand is constantly increasing. There is a need to create a sustainable solution to this problem. Carpooling or ridesharing is a potential solution but there are lot of problems when the context is India. The most important problem is to motivate people to share their ride. The current motivation is the monetary returns which are very small for smaller intra city journeys. The ratio of motivation to problems associated in ridesharing is very low.

Goal

This project tries to trigger or motivate people to share their ride by treating socialisation as a medium. Through the solution, people will be able to find like-minded people with whom they can spend their daily commute time productively. The aim is not just to reduce the traffic on roads by ridesharing but at least making these long hectic traffic journeys productive.

Target Group

For research, the project is scoped down to Hinjewadi IT Park, Pune since Pune has the 4th highest number of people travelling by private vehicles to work and the highest number of people having a travelling distance of 11-20 km in the entire country. In Hinjewadi, around 4 lakh people commute every day from different parts of city. It has around 150 companies and the daily vehicle count on Hinjewadi road is 3.5 lakhs. 44% people travelling here travel by their own private vehicle.
